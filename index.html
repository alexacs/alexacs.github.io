I am currently a Fellow at the Penn Program on Regulation at the University of Pennsylvania Law School. Starting 2016-2017, I will be an Assistant Professor of Political Science at Ohio State University.

Previously I was a Postdoctoral Fellow at the American Academy of Arts and Sciences. I received my PhD from the Department of Politics at Princeton, where I was affiliated with the Center for the Study of Democratic Politics and the Program in Law and Public Affairs.

My research focuses on the political economy of regulation and the challenges of policymaking in modern democracies. I draw upon insights from the fields of positive political theory, organizational economics, administrative law and public administration.
