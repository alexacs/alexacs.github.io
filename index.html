I am currently a Fellow at the Penn Program on Regulation at the University of Pennsylvania Law School. Next academic year I will be an Assistant Professor in the Department of Political Science at Ohio State.

I received my PhD from the Department of Politics at Princeton, where I was affiliated with the Center for the Study of Democratic Politics and the Program in Law and Public Affairs. I spent 2014-15 as a Postdoctoral Fellow at the American Academy of Arts and Sciences

My research focuses on the political economy of regulation and the challenges of policymaking in modern democracies. I draw upon insights from the fields of positive political theory, organizational economics, administrative law and public administration.
